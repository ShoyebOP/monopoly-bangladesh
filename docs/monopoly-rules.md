# Monopoly Rules - Complete Documentation

## Overview
This document contains the complete official rules of Monopoly, including all edge cases and special situations.

---

## Game Objective
Be the last player remaining after all other players have gone bankrupt.

---

## Components

### Board Layout
- **40 Spaces** total around the square board
- **22 Ownable Properties** (colored streets)
- **4 Railroads**
- **2 Utilities** (Electric Company, Water Works)
- **4 Corner Spaces** (Go, Jail/Just Visiting, Free Parking, Go To Jail)
- **2 Tax Spaces** (Income Tax, Luxury Tax)
- **3 Chance Spaces**
- **3 Community Chest Spaces**

### Money Supply
- Total money varies by edition
- Standard US edition includes denominations from $1 to $500

### Cards
- **16 Chance Cards**
- **16 Community Chest Cards**

### Houses and Hotels
- **32 Houses** (green)
- **12 Hotels** (red)

---

## Setup

1. **Banker Selection**: Choose one player to be the banker. The banker manages:
   - Title deeds
   - Houses and hotels
   - Money
   - Auctions

2. **Starting Money**: Each player receives $1,500:
   - 2 × $500
   - 2 × $100
   - 2 × $50
   - 6 × $20
   - 5 × $10
   - 5 × $5
   - 5 × $1

3. **Player Pieces**: Each player chooses a token and places it on Go.

4. **Cards**: Shuffle Chance and Community Chest decks and place them on their designated spaces.

---

## Turn Sequence

On your turn, follow these steps in order:

### 1. Roll Dice
- Roll both six-sided dice
- Move your token clockwise the total number of spaces

### 2. Take Action
The action depends on which space you land on:

#### Ownable Property (unowned)
- You may buy it at the listed price
- If you decline, it goes to auction

#### Ownable Property (owned by another player)
- Pay rent to the owner
- Rent amount depends on:
  - Number of properties in color group owned
  - Number of houses/hotels built

#### Railroad
- Pay rent to owner ($25, $50, $100, or $200 depending on how many railroads owner has)

#### Utility
- Pay rent based on dice roll (4× or 10× dice roll depending on ownership)

#### Chance/Community Chest
- Draw the top card
- Follow the card's instructions immediately
- Return card to bottom of deck unless instructed otherwise

#### Tax Spaces
- **Income Tax**: Pay $200 or 10% of total assets (whichever is higher)
- **Luxury Tax**: Pay $100

#### Go To Jail
- Move directly to Jail space
- Do not pass Go
- Do not collect $200

#### Just Visiting
- No penalty
- You are just visiting, not in jail

#### Free Parking
- No official action (house rules may vary)

### 3. Roll Again (Doubles)
- If you rolled doubles, take another turn
- If you roll doubles three times in a row, go directly to Jail

### 4. End Turn
- Your turn ends when you:
  - Choose not to roll again (no doubles)
  - Go to Jail
  - Complete your third double roll

## Property Rules

### Buying Property
- **When**: When you land on an unowned property
- **Price**: Listed on the board space
- **Process**: Pay bank, receive title deed

### Auction Rules
- **Trigger**: Player lands on unowned property and declines to buy
- **Process**:
  1. Banker auctions property to all players (including the one who declined)
  2. Starting bid can be any amount
  3. Highest bidder wins
  4. Winner pays bank immediately

### Rent Payment
- **When**: Land on property owned by another player
- **Amount**: Determined by:
  - Base rent (unimproved)
  - Rent with 1-4 houses
  - Rent with hotel
  - Monopoly multiplier (if owner has all properties in color group)

### Building Houses/Hotels
- **Requirement**: Must own all properties in color group
- **Order**: Must build evenly across properties
  - Cannot build second house on one property until all have one house
  - Same for 3rd, 4th houses
- **Cost**: Varies by property group (see Board Spaces document)
- **Hotels**: Replace 4 houses with 1 hotel

### Mortgaging Property
- **When**: Any time during your turn or between turns
- **Amount**: Half the purchase price (listed on title deed)
- **Effect**:
  - Cannot collect rent while mortgaged
  - Still owned by player
- **Unmortgaging**:
  - Pay mortgage value + 10% interest
  - Must be done on your turn before rolling

## Jail Rules

### How You Go To Jail
1. Land on "Go To Jail" space
2. Roll doubles three times in a row
3. Draw a Chance/Community Chest card that sends you to jail

### While In Jail
- Cannot move (skip turns)
- Can still:
  - Buy and sell properties
  - Buy and sell houses/hotels
  - Collect rent
  - Participate in auctions

### Getting Out of Jail
You have three options:

#### Option 1: Pay Fine
- Pay $50
- Roll dice and move normally on same turn

#### Option 2: Use Get Out of Jail Free Card
- Use your own card or buy from another player
- Move on same turn

#### Option 3: Roll Doubles
- Attempt on your turn
- If successful, move and take normal turn
- If failed after 3 attempts, must pay $50 and move

### Maximum Turns in Jail
- 3 turns maximum
- After 3rd failed attempt, must pay $50 and move

---

## Trading Rules

### What Can Be Traded
- Properties (owned)
- Money
- Get Out of Jail Free cards

### Trading Restrictions
- Cannot trade with players in jail (except Get Out of Jail Free cards)
- Cannot trade properties with houses/hotels (must sell back first)
- All trades must be voluntary

### Trade Process
1. Propose trade to another player
2. Both players agree
3. Exchange items immediately
4. Trade is final

---

## Bankruptcy Rules

### When You Go Bankrupt
- Cannot pay what you owe (to bank or player)

### Bankruptcy to Bank
- Turn over all assets to bank
- Bank auctions all properties immediately
- Game continues for remaining players

### Bankruptcy to Player
- Turn over all assets to creditor
- Creditor receives:
  - All properties (mortgaged and unmortgaged)
  - All buildings (must be sold or kept)
  - All Get Out of Jail Free cards
- Creditor must pay any debts owed by bankrupt player first

### Immediate Bankruptcy
- If you cannot pay immediately, you are bankrupt
- No payment plans allowed

---

## Winning the Game

### Standard Win Condition
- Last player remaining after all others go bankrupt

### Alternative Win Conditions (House Rules)
- Most money after predetermined time limit
- First player to bankrupt all opponents
- First player to own all properties

---

## Special Rules & Edge Cases

### Running Out of Houses
- If bank runs out of houses, players cannot build
- Must wait for houses to be returned (via sales or bankruptcy)
- Hotels can still be built if houses available to convert

### Money Shortage
- If bank runs out of money, banker can create more
- Use paper/phone to track additional money

### Property Disputes
- Rent must be requested before next player rolls
- If not requested, rent is forfeited for that landing

### Dice Roll Errors
- If wrong roll is made, correct before next roll
- If discovered later, play continues from error

### Card Errors
- If wrong card drawn, return and reshuffle if not read
- If read, card must be followed

### Simultaneous Actions
- Turn order is always clockwise
- No simultaneous actions in standard rules

---

## End of Game

### Game End Triggers
1. Only one player remains solvent
2. Predetermined time limit reached (house rule)
3. All properties owned and developed (rare)

### Final Rankings
1. Last player standing (winner)
2. Second-to-last bankrupt (2nd place)
3. And so on...

---

## Official vs House Rules

### Common House Rules (Not Official)
- **Free Parking Jackpot**: Money from taxes goes to Free Parking
- **Salary Bonus for Landing on Go**: Collect double salary
- **No Auction**: Properties go back to bank if declined
- **Unlimited Houses**: Can build without even distribution
- **Trading During Jail**: Can trade while in jail

### Recommendation
- Use only official rules for first play
- House rules can be added for variety after mastering official rules

---

## Quick Reference

### Most Important Rules
1. Roll dice and move clockwise
2. Buy unowned properties or auction them
3. Pay rent when landing on owned properties
4. Build houses/hotels to increase rent
5. Go to Jail if you land on Go To Jail space
6. Bankruptcy eliminates you from the game
7. Last player standing wins

### Common Mistakes to Avoid
1. Forgetting to collect $200 when passing Go
2. Not paying rent when required
3. Building houses unevenly
4. Trading while in jail (not allowed in official rules)
5. Forgetting to auction declined properties
